1. PROPOSAL REVIEW
------------------
Ifeoma walked the team through the proposed methods/tech stack:
- Move away from composite scoring to advanced ML models
- Use Google Gemini instead of GPT-4o for conversational AI
- GEE for real-time satellite data extraction
- GBM and XGBoost for fine-scale malaria risk modeling
- MaxEnt and BioMod2 for Anopheles habitat suitability
- LangChain and FAISS for RAG (retrieval-augmented generation)
- PuLP and Google OR-Tools for building-level allocation optimization
- TensorFlow CNNs for settlement classification
- React Native for offline-capable mobile deployment
- FastAPI on GCP

Ifeoma's vision: clinical malaria data + field study data + satellite
data -> ML risk model -> fine-scale maps. Composite scoring remains as
a parallel output for government validation against the ML maps.

Integer programming optimization: optimize net allocation at building
level given budget constraints and household data from campaigns.

RAG: train on Nigerian malaria research + policy documents for locally
relevant LLM responses.

Eniola asked about clarity on planned extensions in proposal (100-word
limit is tight). Ifeoma confirmed the methodology section needs more
space than available.

Ifeoma noted DSN Nigeria just released their own DHIS2 chatbot
(released March 18, 2026) - space is getting competitive.
