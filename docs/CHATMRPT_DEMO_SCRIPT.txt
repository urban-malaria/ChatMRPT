================================================================================
                    CHATMRPT DEMO SCRIPT (15 MINUTES)
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPENING: WHY CHATMRPT? (2 minutes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Show title slide or ChatMRPT homepage]

"Every year, malaria programs collect massive amounts of data - from health
facilities, surveys, and environmental sources. The challenge? Turning that
data into actionable decisions.

Traditional analysis requires specialized skills - GIS experts, statisticians,
data scientists. This creates bottlenecks. Program managers wait weeks for
analysis. By the time insights arrive, the situation on the ground may have
changed.

ChatMRPT changes this.

It's an AI-powered malaria epidemiologist that lets anyone - program managers,
M&E officers, district health teams - ask questions in plain English and get
answers immediately.

It can do two things: answer general questions about malaria, AND analyze your
program data. Let me show you both."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE INTERFACE (1 minute)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Show the main ChatMRPT interface]

"This is ChatMRPT. Notice it looks like a chat interface - familiar, simple.
No complex menus or buttons to learn.

You simply type what you want to know, and ChatMRPT responds."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE ARENA: KNOWLEDGE QUESTIONS (2 minutes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARENA QUESTION 1:
[Type: "Why are children under 5 most vulnerable to malaria?"]

"Let's start with a question relevant to every malaria program.

[Two responses appear side by side]

This is the Arena - ChatMRPT uses two different AI models competing to give
you the best answer. Response A and Response B side by side.

Both explain that young children haven't developed immunity yet - they haven't
had repeated exposures that build partial protection. This is why under-5
mortality is our key indicator.

[Vote for the better response]

You vote for the better answer. This helps improve the system over time."


ARENA QUESTION 2:
[Type: "How does rainfall affect malaria transmission?"]

"This connects to something we'll see later in our analysis.

[Arena responses appear]

Rainfall creates breeding sites for mosquitoes - but the relationship isn't
simple. Too little rain means no breeding sites. Too much rain can wash away
larvae. There's a sweet spot that drives transmission peaks.

This is why ChatMRPT includes rainfall data in risk analysis - it's a key
predictor of where outbreaks will occur."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRANSITION TO DATA ANALYSIS (30 seconds)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

"That's ChatMRPT as a knowledge assistant. But the real power comes when you
bring your own data.

Let me show you how ChatMRPT transforms raw program data into actionable
insights."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1: UPLOAD DATA (1 minute)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Click upload button, select your test file]

"I'll upload routine health facility data - monthly reports showing tests
conducted and positive cases. This is data every malaria program has.

[Upload file]

ChatMRPT automatically detects what kind of data this is and what analysis
is possible."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2: START TPR WORKFLOW (1.5 minutes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Type: "start the tpr workflow"]

"I'll ask ChatMRPT to calculate malaria burden.

[Select: Primary facilities, Under-5 children]

I'm focusing on primary health facilities and children under 5 - this shows
true community burden, not referral cases from hospitals.

Why 'burden per 1,000'? Instead of just percentages, ChatMRPT calculates
cases per 1,000 children in each ward. A small ward with 50% positivity might
have fewer actual cases than a large ward with 30%. This metric accounts for
population - so we're comparing apples to apples when allocating resources."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3: MAP MALARIA BURDEN (1.5 minutes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Type: "map malaria burden distribution"]

"Now let's visualize where the burden is highest.

[Map appears]

This choropleth map instantly shows the geographic pattern. Darker colors
mean higher burden.

Notice the clusters - malaria isn't randomly distributed. There are geographic
patterns driven by environment and health system factors.

One sentence, instant map. This would typically take hours in GIS software."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 4: RUN RISK ANALYSIS (1.5 minutes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Type: "run malaria risk analysis"]

"Test positivity tells us where malaria IS today. For planning, we need to
know where it WILL BE - and why.

ChatMRPT combines your health data with environmental factors - rainfall,
proximity to water bodies, elevation, urbanization - to create a comprehensive
risk score.

[Results appear]

Now every ward has a risk score and ranking. The system identifies which
factors drive risk in each area.

Ward A might be high-risk due to proximity to water bodies. Ward B might be
high-risk due to low elevation and high rainfall. Different drivers require
different interventions."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 5: VULNERABILITY MAP (1.5 minutes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Type: "create composite vulnerability map"]

"Let's visualize these risk rankings.

[Map appears with risk categories]

Wards classified as High Risk, Medium Risk, Low Risk. All our analysis
combined into one actionable view.

Program managers can immediately see: these are the wards that need priority
attention.

[Type: "What are the top 10 highest risk wards?"]

And if I want specifics - here's the ranked list with scores. Ready for
your program planning meeting."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 6: ITN PLANNING (1.5 minutes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Type: "I want to plan bed net distribution"]

"Now the real power - turning analysis into action.

I have 2 million insecticide-treated nets to distribute across this state.

[Type: "I have 2 million nets and average household size is 4"]

[Results and map appear]

Instantly, ChatMRPT creates a distribution plan that:
  - Prioritizes high-risk wards first
  - Calculates nets needed per ward based on population
  - Shows which wards get full coverage vs partial allocation
  - Generates a map showing the distribution strategy

This planning would typically take days of spreadsheet work. ChatMRPT does
it in seconds - and it's based on the risk analysis we just completed."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The traditional way: weeks of work across multiple specialists

Questions?"
